A: "Felt Table"

Balatro-closest. Deep green felt bg with vignette. Amber/gold chrome everywhere. Nodes are raised casino chips with radial gradients + depth shadows. Left sidebar for HUD. Warm, rich, tactile.

Pros

  • Most cohesive atmosphere
  • Strongest "indie roguelike" identity
  • Warm palette reduces eye strain

Cons

  • Green bg may clash with Grass-type nodes
  • Heavy atmosphere could distract from sprites
  • Furthest from Pokemon brand colors

B: "Mystic Cards"

Pokemon Ghost/Psychic meets Balatro cards. Deep purple-black bg. Nodes are mini cards with gradient fills, purple chrome, and neon glow. Labels ON the cards (not tooltips). Mysterious, arcane, elegant.

Pros

  • Card shape gives room for labels
  • Purple ties to Ghost/Psychic types — very Pokemon
  • Neon glow is distinctive and modern

Cons

  • Purple may not feel "warm" enough
  • Card shapes take more space than circles
  • Less retro, more stylized

C: "Pokemon Red Cartridge"

Lean into Pokemon Red identity hard. Dark red-black bg with CRT scanlines. Red chrome and red neon glow. Circles with red borders. Bottom HUD bar like a Game Boy status line. Nostalgic, intense, branded.

Pros

  • Instantly recognizable as Pokemon
  • CRT scanlines reinforce retro feel
  • Red-on-dark is high contrast and striking

Cons

  • Red fatigue on long play sessions
  • Monochrome chrome limits visual variety
  • May feel oppressive without warmth variation

D: "Warm Sidebar" (Recommended)

Best of all proposals. Warm green-dark bg with vignette (color proposal). Amber chrome (typography). Casino chip nodes with gradients (map proposal). Sidebar layout (layout proposal). Smooth hover + stepped pixel anims (animation). Per-screen atmosphere (atmosphere). The full synthesis.

Pros

  • Combines strongest element from each proposal
  • Sidebar uses horizontal space well
  • Warm but not overwhelming — Pokemon-native feel
  • Room for info without labels on nodes

Cons

  • Most implementation work (touches everything)
  • Sidebar may feel cramped on smaller screens

Shared across all approaches

  • HTML tooltip portal (not SVG title)
  • Smooth hover (150ms ease-out) on interactive elements
  • steps() only for HP drain, typewriter, damage shake
  • Node radial gradients for depth
  • Breathing pulse (not blinking) for accessible nodes
  • Visited nodes keep identity (desaturated, not faded)
  • Press Start 2P for display only, VT323 for body
  • image-rendering: pixelated on all sprites
  • Vignette on all screens
  • prefers-reduced-motion respected
